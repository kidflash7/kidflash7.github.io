Wrangell-St. Elias is the biggest national park in the country. It has a and acrage of 8,323,148 and it was established as a national park in December of 1980. The park is found in Alaska. Three of the most popular sites in Wrangell-St. Elias are Mount Blackburn, Nabesna Road, Malaspina Glacier. WSE occupies more than 8 million acres along the Canadian border. Unlike many Alaskan national parks, Wrangell-St. Elias is accessible by vehicle, though most of its roads are gravel.
